# Clippings

Web clippings, read-later material, and saved articles — the reference backlog awaiting attention. This area holds material clipped from the web or saved for later reading: articles, blog posts, social media captures, and any external content preserved for future reference. Clippings are inbound information, not yet processed.

## What belongs here

- Web article clippings and full-page saves
- Blog posts and essays saved for later reading
- Social media posts or threads worth preserving
- Read-later items queued for processing
- Reference material captured from external sources without annotation

## What does NOT belong here

- Source material tied to active research → [[../../1. Study/Sources/README.md|Sources]]
- Personal notes or reflections → [[../../0. Gatehouse/Daily/README.md|Daily]] or [[../../1. Study/Thought Streams/README.md|Thought Streams]]
- Completed project records → [[../Completed Projects/README.md|Completed Projects]]
- Obsolete content with no remaining value → [[../Obsolete/README.md|Obsolete]]

## Tips

- **Preserve source metadata.** Every clipping must include the original URL, the date captured, and (if available) the author and publication. Clippings without provenance degrade quickly in value.
- **Process or prune.** Clippings should be reviewed periodically. Read, annotate, and promote valuable ones to [[../../1. Study/Sources/README.md|Sources]] or [[../../2. Library/General Reference/README.md|General Reference]]. Discard the rest.
- **No editing clippings.** The content of a clipping is the source material. Do not modify clipped text. Annotations and commentary go in a separate section or linked note.
- **Date of capture matters.** The capture date is as important as the publication date. Material from 2020 captured in 2025 carries different context than material from 2025 captured in 2025.
