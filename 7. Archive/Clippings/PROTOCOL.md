# Clippings

Web clippings, read-later material, and saved articles — the reference backlog awaiting attention.

## What belongs here

- Web article clippings and full-page saves
- Blog posts and essays saved for later reading
- Social media posts or threads worth preserving
- Read-later items queued for processing
- Reference material captured from external sources without annotation

## What does NOT belong here

- Source material tied to active research (move to [[../../1. Study/Sources/PROTOCOL|Sources Protocol]])
- Personal notes or reflections (those go to [[../../5. Quarters/Living/PROTOCOL|Living Protocol]] or [[../../1. Study/Thought Streams/PROTOCOL|Thought Streams Protocol]])
- Completed project records (those go to [[../Completed Projects/PROTOCOL|Completed Projects Protocol]])
- Obsolete content with no remaining value (those go to [[../Obsolete/PROTOCOL|Obsolete Protocol]])

## Area-specific rules

1. **Preserve source metadata.** Every clipping must include the original URL, the date captured, and (if available) the author and publication. Clippings without provenance degrade quickly in value.
2. **No editing clippings.** The content of a clipping is the source material. Do not modify clipped text. Annotations and commentary go in a separate section or linked note.
3. **LLM indexing.** The LLM may tag, categorize, and create metadata for clippings to improve retrieval. It may also summarize clippings, but summaries must be clearly marked as LLM-generated.
