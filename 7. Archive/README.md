# The Archive

Completed work, historical records, anything definitively finished. The Archive exists so active rooms do not accumulate dead weight. No new thinking happens here. Everything is frozen -- archived notes are never edited. If something becomes relevant again, you create a new note in an active room that links back to the archived original.

## How this room works

PRECISION-RETRIEVAL mode, LLM-augmented substrate. The Indexer operates on frozen content. No tacit understanding can be displaced because the material is no longer being developed. The Indexer tags and cross-references archived material, surfaces relevant content when similar situations arise in active rooms, maintains archive MoCs and indexes, and generates finding aids. It does not modify archived content or reopen archived projects.

## What you do here

- **The Archive is frozen.** Do not edit archived notes. If something becomes relevant again, create a new note in an active room that links back to the archived original.
- **Browse the Archive when you need historical context.** The Indexer makes it searchable.
- **Completed projects retain their full project record** from the Workshop or Gallery. The walking path is preserved.
- **Clippings that are not actively being used should go here**, not in the Library. If you start actively working with a clipping, promote it to the Study or Library.
- **Obsolete content is kept for the record.** It may be deleted after a reasonable retention period, but never modified.

## What moves out

- Archived content does not leave the Archive in its original form
- If something becomes relevant again, a new note is created in an active room that links back to the archived original
- Clippings being actively worked with may be promoted to the Study or Library as new notes
