# The Gallery

Work in motion: job searches, active business operations, deliverables in progress. Items enter when a project starts and exit when it completes. The Gallery never accumulates permanent residents -- if something has been here for months with no activity, it probably belongs in the Archive.

## How this room works

BRIDGE mode, dual-substrate. The Project Coordinator handles operational overhead while you focus on judgment and execution. The LLM tracks deadlines, drafts correspondence, maintains status summaries, and schedules meetings. It does not make strategic career or business decisions, send anything under your name without review, or modify project scope on its own. Coordination work is explicit and mechanical -- perfect LLM territory. Strategic work is tacit and judgmental -- strictly human.

## What you do here

- **The Gallery is for active work.** When a project completes, it leaves (to Archive or Library).
- **Review the Gallery regularly.** Stalled projects should be paused (move to status: waiting) or closed (move to Archive).
- **Correspondence that goes out under your name must be reviewed by you.** The Coordinator drafts; you approve.
- **Opportunities that expire should be archived**, not left to clutter.
- **The Gallery is transient by nature.** If something has been here for months with no activity, it probably belongs in Archive.

## What moves out

- Completed projects migrate to Archive, retaining their full project record
- Project documentation that becomes reference material may migrate to the Library
- Expired opportunities migrate to Archive
- Sent and final correspondence can be archived
