# Finance

Personal financial planning, budgets, and monetary records — the household ledger. This area holds personal financial notes: budgets, spending records, savings goals, investment notes, and financial planning documents. Finance is operational and sensitive; it supports decisions without replacing professional advice.

## What belongs here

- Budget worksheets and spending logs
- Savings and investment notes
- Financial goal tracking
- Insurance and policy records
- Major purchase records and depreciation notes
- Personal financial planning documents

## What does NOT belong here

- Business/freelance accounting → [[../../6. Counting House/Accounts/README.md|Accounts]]
- Invoices to clients → [[../../6. Counting House/Invoicing/README.md|Invoicing]]
- Tax filings → [[../../6. Counting House/Tax/README.md|Tax]]
- Legal contracts → [[../../6. Counting House/Legal/README.md|Legal]]
- Health expenses without financial context → [[../Health/README.md|Health]]

## Tips

- **No account credentials.** Do not store bank logins, PINs, or passwords here. This is a planning area, not a credential vault.
- **Currency and date.** Always specify the currency and the date for any financial figure. "500" means nothing without context.
- **Separate personal and business.** Personal finances live here; business finances live in the Counting House. If the line blurs, note the overlap explicitly.
- **Review cycle.** Financial notes should be reviewed at least quarterly. Budgets and goals that are never revisited are not useful.
