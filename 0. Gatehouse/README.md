# The Gatehouse

Everything enters here first. Some of it moves to other rooms. Some ages into archive. The Gatehouse is transient by design -- nothing lives here long-term. It is the place where incoming material lands before it has been sorted, the daily note you write this morning, the deadline you need to remember next week.

## How this room works

Capture mode, human-primary substrate. The LLM acts as a Scribe -- it captures what comes in, suggests where it might belong, and handles the mechanical parts of recording. It does not shape, summarize, or interpret your material. You decide what something means and where it goes.

## What you do here

- **Capture first, classify later.** Do not worry about where something goes when you are capturing it. Drop it in the Inbox and move on.
- **Review your Inbox regularly.** Items that sit in Inbox for more than a week should be classified or archived.
- **Daily notes are for today.** If a daily note becomes reference material, move it to the Library.
- **Calendar items that expire should be archived**, not left to clutter.

## What moves out

- Inbox items that have been triaged migrate to their proper room (Study, Library, Workshop, Gallery, etc.)
- Daily notes that become reference material migrate to the Library
- Expired calendar items migrate to the Archive
- Anything that sits too long without action gets classified or archived
