# The Counting House

Financial records, tax documents, invoicing, receipts, corporate documents, legal materials. Pure precision. Nothing exploratory. Everything is a record. The Clerk handles the mechanical heart of bookkeeping -- you review, you approve, you decide. Financial records are pure explicit data; there is almost no tacit knowledge embedded in a receipt or an invoice, so the LLM can operate freely without displacement risk.

## How this room works

PRECISION mode, LLM-augmented substrate. The Clerk organizes records, generates invoices and summaries, flags anomalies and overdue items, cross-references tax documents, and produces financial statements. It does not make financial decisions (those are judgment calls), submit regulatory filings autonomously, or waive invoices without approval. The Clerk processes what happened. It does not try to understand why.

## What you do here

- **All financial and legal records go here.** Personal financial planning stays in Quarters/Finance.
- **The Clerk can do most of the bookkeeping.** You review, you approve, you decide.
- **Regulatory filings always require your sign-off.** The Clerk prepares; you submit.
- **Legal documents are records.** If you need to analyze a legal situation, that analysis belongs in the Study (or see a lawyer).
- **Keep records by year.** The Clerk can organize, but the year-based structure is the load-bearing beam.

## What moves out

- Legal analysis that requires thinking moves to the Study
- Personal financial planning topics belong in Quarters/Finance
- Historical records that are no longer actively referenced migrate to Archive
