# Tax

Tax filings and supporting documents — the compliance record for governmental obligations. This area holds tax-related records: filed returns, supporting schedules, correspondence with tax authorities, and planning notes for upcoming filings. Tax records are legal documents; accuracy and retention are paramount.

## What belongs here

- Filed tax returns and supporting schedules
- Correspondence with tax authorities
- Tax planning notes and projections
- Deduction and credit documentation
- Year-end tax preparation checklists

## What does NOT belong here

- General financial records → [[../Accounts/README.md|Accounts]]
- Invoices → [[../Invoicing/README.md|Invoicing]]
- Contracts → [[../Legal/README.md|Legal]]
- Personal financial planning → [[../../5. Quarters/Finance/README.md|Finance]]
- Regulatory filings unrelated to tax → [[../Compliance/README.md|Compliance]]

## Tips

- **Jurisdiction and year.** Organize by tax jurisdiction and fiscal year: `Jurisdiction/FY-YYYY/`. This structure mirrors how filings are submitted.
- **Retain everything.** Tax records must be retained for the legally mandated period (typically 7 years, varies by jurisdiction). Do not delete or archive prematurely.
- **Precision is non-negotiable.** All figures on filed returns must be human-verified.
- **Supporting documents linked.** Every line item on a tax return should have its supporting document either linked or filed alongside.
- **Mark filing status.** Use `status: draft`, `status: filed`, `status: amended` for each return. This prevents accidental reuse of draft figures.
