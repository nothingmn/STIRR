# Invoicing

Client invoicing records — what was billed, to whom, and whether it was paid. This area holds all invoicing records: invoices sent, payment tracking, and outstanding receivables. It is the Counting House's outgoing bill register.

## What belongs here

- Invoices issued to clients (by number and date)
- Payment receipts and confirmation records
- Outstanding receivables and aging reports
- Invoice templates and rate schedules
- Write-off and bad debt records

## What does NOT belong here

- General account balances → [[../Accounts/README.md|Accounts]]
- Tax records → [[../Tax/README.md|Tax]]
- Contracts → [[../Legal/README.md|Legal]]
- Expense records not tied to invoicing → [[../Accounts/README.md|Accounts]]
- Correspondence about invoices → [[../../4. Gallery/Correspondence/README.md|Correspondence]]

## Tips

- **Sequential numbering.** Every invoice gets a unique sequential number. Do not reuse numbers or leave gaps without explanation.
- **Track payment status.** Every invoice record must include its payment status: `issued`, `paid`, `overdue`, `written_off`.
- **Date rigor.** Record the invoice date, due date, and (when paid) the payment date. These drive cash flow analysis and tax preparation.
- **Link to accounts.** Paid invoices should link to the corresponding Account entry.
- **Retain all invoices.** Even voided and written-off invoices remain here with their status. The paper trail must be complete.
