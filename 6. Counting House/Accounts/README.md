# Accounts

Financial accounts organized by entity or fiscal year — the Counting House's ledger. This area holds business and freelance financial records organized by entity or fiscal year: income, expenses, account balances, and financial statements. It is the operational core of the Counting House.

## What belongs here

- Income and expense records by entity or fiscal year
- Bank and payment account summaries
- Financial statements and reconciliation notes
- Budget vs. actual tracking for business entities
- Cash flow records and projections

## What does NOT belong here

- Client invoices → [[../Invoicing/README.md|Invoicing]]
- Tax filings and supporting documents → [[../Tax/README.md|Tax]]
- Contracts and legal documents → [[../Legal/README.md|Legal]]
- Personal financial records → [[../../5. Quarters/Finance/README.md|Finance]]
- Regulatory filings → [[../Compliance/README.md|Compliance]]

## Tips

- **Organize by entity or year.** Use a consistent structure: either by entity (`EntityName/`) or by fiscal year (`FY-YYYY/`). Do not mix both in a single directory level.
- **Currency and date mandatory.** Every financial figure must include its currency and the date it applies to. Ambiguity in financial records is unacceptable.
- **Reconcile regularly.** Account records should be reconciled against bank statements at least monthly. Flag discrepancies for human resolution.
- **Retain records.** Do not delete account records, even for closed periods. They support tax preparation, audit, and historical analysis.
