# Sources

Source material, references, and bibliographic records — the evidentiary foundation of the Study.

## What belongs here

- Bibliographic records for books, papers, and articles
- Annotations and marginalia tied to specific sources
- PDFs or transcriptions of key source material (or links to them)
- Conversation logs that serve as primary sources
- Structured reference entries with metadata (author, date, DOI, etc.)

## What does NOT belong here

- Analysis or argument built from sources (that belongs in [[../Research/PROTOCOL|Research Protocol]])
- Web clippings without academic or research value (those go to [[../../7. Archive/Clippings/PROTOCOL|Clippings Protocol]])
- General reference on stable topics (those go to [[../../2. Library/General Reference/PROTOCOL|General Reference Protocol]])
- Drafts of your own writing (those go to [[../Writing/PROTOCOL|Writing Protocol]])

## Area-specific rules

1. **One source, one note.** Each source gets its own file. Do not combine multiple sources into a single entry.
2. **Guard annotations.** The LLM may help format citations and retrieve metadata, but annotations — your reading of a source — are human territory. The LLM does not annotate on your behalf.
3. **Link up.** Source notes should be linked from every research or synthesis note that draws on them. Downstream notes depend on these links for provenance.
