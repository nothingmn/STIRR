# Synthesis

Cross-domain connections and integrated insight — where separate threads are woven together. This area holds notes that connect ideas across different domains, projects, or research threads. Synthesis is the integrative layer of the Study, where patterns are recognized and frameworks are built from disparate parts.

## What belongs here

- Notes that bridge two or more research threads
- Conceptual frameworks and mental models
- "Connection" notes linking ideas from different domains
- Summaries that integrate findings from multiple sources
- Meta-analyses and pattern-level observations

## What does NOT belong here

- Single-domain research notes → [[../Research/README.md|Research]]
- Drafts of essays → [[../Writing/README.md|Writing]]
- Unprocessed raw ideas → [[../Thought Streams/README.md|Thought Streams]]
- Reference data → [[../Sources/README.md|Sources]] or [[../../2. Library/General Reference/README.md|General Reference]]

## Tips

- **Multiple links required.** A synthesis note that connects to only one source is not synthesis — it is a summary. Every synthesis note should link outward to at least two distinct threads or domains.
- **Name by pattern.** Title synthesis notes after the pattern or connection they describe, not after a single topic. Favor "X and Y" or "On the relationship between..." structures.
- **Mark confidence.** Use a `confidence` property or tag to indicate how well-supported a synthesis is: `speculative`, `emerging`, `supported`.
- **Revisit and revise.** Synthesis naturally evolves as new information arrives. Revisit existing synthesis notes when linked research updates.
