# The Study

The most important room for intellectual life. Work that does not yet have a fixed shape lives here: research in progress, essays being written, cross-domain connections emerging. The Study tolerates incomplete notes, contradictions, and two drafts of the same idea side by side. Premature precision would kill the thinking.

## How this room works

RANGE mode, human-primary substrate. The LLM acts as an Interlocutor with guarded access -- it can ask you questions, surface connections, and offer alternative framings, but it must not do your thinking for you. It may not summarize without request, draft your arguments, resolve your contradictions, or reorganize your notes. The boundary question: is the LLM doing the holistic thinking FOR you (displacing) or ALONGSIDE you (augmenting on a different substrate)?

## What you do here

- **Think here.** This is your workspace for developing ideas.
- **Do not let the LLM "clean up" your thinking.** Mess is a feature. Contradictions are features.
- **When a piece of thinking becomes structured enough for retrieval, it migrates to the Library.** The Interlocutor can suggest when this is appropriate, but you decide.
- **Follow the Attempt-Before-Documentation Rule:** before writing detailed notes, attempt to explain the concept from memory. Then attempt to apply it. Then write.
- **The Three-Pass Reading Process:** first pass holistic (no notes), second pass artifact capture, third pass analytic.

## What moves out

- Thinking that becomes structured enough for retrieval migrates to the Library
- Source material that has been fully analyzed may also move to the Library
- Cross-domain connections that crystallize into structured knowledge migrate to the Library
