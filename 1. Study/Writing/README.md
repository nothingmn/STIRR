# Writing

Essays, articles, and drafts in progress — the place where thinking becomes prose. This area holds written work at various stages of completion: early drafts, revisions, published pieces, and abandoned drafts worth preserving. Writing is the outward form the Study's thinking takes.

## What belongs here

- Drafts of essays, articles, and papers
- Revision histories and editorial notes
- Published pieces (with a `status: published` property)
- Outlines and structural skeletons for planned works
- Abandoned drafts retained for salvage value

## What does NOT belong here

- Raw research notes → [[../Research/README.md|Research]]
- Half-formed ideas without narrative structure → [[../Thought Streams/README.md|Thought Streams]]
- Correspondence and letters → [[../../4. Gallery/Correspondence/README.md|Correspondence]]
- Source material and references → [[../Sources/README.md|Sources]]

## Tips

- **Track status.** Every piece should carry a status: `draft`, `revision`, `published`, or `abandoned`.
- **One piece, one file.** Each essay or article gets its own file. Do not bundle multiple works in a single document.
- **Preserve revisions.** When a draft undergoes significant change, consider keeping a prior version rather than overwriting — particularly for long-form work.
- **Cite sources.** Link back to research notes and source material. The chain of evidence should be traceable.
